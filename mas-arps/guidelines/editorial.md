# MAS-ARPS Editorial Guidelines
## University-Level Academic Presentation Standard

---

# 1. Overall Academic Standards

## 1.1 Academic Tone
- Use formal, objective, and precise language.
- Avoid informal phrasing and conversational tone.
- Avoid exaggerated or speculative claims unless clearly labeled.
- Do not use emotional or persuasive language.
- Use domain-specific terminology appropriately.

## 1.2 Evidence-Based Content
- Every major claim must be supported by at least one credible academic source.
- Sources must be traceable (author, year, title, publication).
- If conflicting findings exist, acknowledge them.
- Distinguish clearly between established theory and emerging research.

## 1.3 Logical Flow
Presentations must follow a coherent academic structure:

1. Title Slide
2. Learning Objectives
3. Background / Context
4. Literature Review
5. Core Concepts / Theory
6. Analysis / Discussion
7. Applications / Case Studies (if applicable)
8. Limitations
9. Future Directions
10. Conclusion
11. References

---

# 2. Slide Formatting Standards

## 2.1 Bullet Rules
- Maximum 6 bullets per slide.
- Maximum 12–15 words per bullet.
- No full paragraphs on slides.
- Maintain parallel grammatical structure across bullets.
- Each slide should communicate one main idea.

### Example (Correct)
- Quantum algorithms exploit superposition.
- Shor’s algorithm factors integers efficiently.
- Security implications affect RSA systems.

### Example (Incorrect)
- Quantum computing is a very interesting and revolutionary technology that might change everything in the future.

---

## 2.2 Slide Density
- Avoid overcrowding slides.
- Use diagrams or figures when they clarify complex concepts.
- Avoid decorative or irrelevant images.

---

## 2.3 Slide Titles
- Titles must be informative and descriptive.
- Avoid vague titles.

### Good Examples
- Limitations of Current Post-Quantum Schemes
- Machine Learning Models in Climate Forecasting

### Bad Examples
- More Information
- Details

---

# 3. Citation Standards

## 3.1 Default Citation Style
APA (7th Edition)

## 3.2 In-Text Citation Format (On Slides)
- (Smith, 2022)
- (Johnson & Lee, 2021)

## 3.3 Reference Slide Format (APA Example)
Smith, J. (2022). *Quantum cryptography methods*. Journal of Security Studies, 14(2), 122–135.

## 3.4 Citation Rules
- At least one citation per conceptual slide.
- All in-text citations must appear on the reference slide.
- Reference slide must be alphabetically ordered.
- Do not fabricate citations.
- Do not cite non-existent journals.

---

# 4. Speaker Notes Standards

- Speaker notes must expand slide bullets into full academic explanations.
- Write in complete sentences.
- Include definitions, examples, and clarifications.
- Maintain academic tone.
- Notes may exceed slide bullet length.
- Include citations in notes when expanding on research findings.

---

# 5. Visual and Diagram Standards

- Visuals must support learning objectives.
- All figures must include labels.
- Cite the original source if adapted from literature.
- Avoid decorative visuals without instructional value.

---

# 6. Content Quality Requirements

## 6.1 Accuracy
- All claims must be supported by retrieved sources.
- Unsupported claims are not permitted.

## 6.2 Balance
- If the topic is controversial, present multiple perspectives.

## 6.3 Depth
University-level presentations must include:
- Definitions
- Theoretical foundations
- Mechanisms or processes
- Current research developments
- Limitations and gaps

Avoid superficial summaries.

---

# 7. Ethical and Academic Integrity Standards

- No fabricated references.
- No misleading interpretation of findings.
- Clearly distinguish between:
  - Empirical findings
  - Theoretical claims
  - Author interpretation
- Respect copyright and citation norms.

---

# 8. Length and Scope Standards

For a 45–60 minute lecture:

- 12–20 slides total.
- 1–2 minutes per slide.
- Minimum 3–6 academic sources.
- Recommended 8–15 references for research-intensive topics.

---

# 9. Writing Style Rules

Use:
- Precise terminology.
- Clear conceptual hierarchy.
- Active voice where appropriate.
- Concise phrasing.

Avoid:
- Redundant language.
- Emotional adjectives.
- Marketing tone.
- Overly complex sentence structures.

---

# 10. Pre-Export Validation Checklist

Before exporting the presentation, verify:

- No slide exceeds 6 bullets.
- No bullet exceeds 15 words.
- Each slide has a descriptive title.
- Each major slide contains at least one citation.
- A complete reference slide exists.
- References are alphabetically ordered (APA).
- Speaker notes exist for each slide.
- No placeholder or incomplete text remains.
